Who is the brains of this operation?

My name is Andrew McGuire. Despite there only being one person at Cliste, I'm going to use the Royal "We" when talking about the company. It just sounds classier.

What's your background?

I went to Drake University in Des Moines, Iowa, where I got a Bachelor's in history, with minors in math and geography. After that, I spent a year living in Dublin, Ireland while I got my Master's in Irish history. A few odd jobs here and there - teaching English in Prague, driving a Zamboni in Buffalo - and I found myself in Austin. Tired of the odd jobs, I decided I wanted to strike out on my own, so I took some web development courses and here we are.

How do I pronounce "cliste"?

/KLISH-tah/

What does it mean?

Cliste is an Irish word meaning "smart" or "clever". When I was looking for a name for my company, I decided I wanted an Irish word, both to celebrate my heritage and education, but also because it was pretty much guaranteed to be unique. When I found cliste in the dictionary, I knew right away it was the right word.

What can Cliste do for me?

We specialize in web development, specifically the architecture of your new website. We'll do our absolute best to make your website do exactly what you want it to do in terms of functionality.

So you won't make me a logo?

While we can design layouts, it's not our forte. We're more math-brained than art-brained. We'll do our best to design any components you need, but if you want a really, really nice logo drawn up, I suggest you give my friend Lindsey a call. She's the graphic design badass at Creens.com

This project is still in development. So far, accomplishments include:
  • loading external RSS feeds
  • incorporating HTML5 <audio> tags
  • pausing and restarting playback
  • use of localStorage to save playlists after browser close
Further improvements needed include:
